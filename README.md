# AI Job Market Data Analysis (Excel Project)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a **synthetic yet realistic snapshot of the modern AI-driven job market** to uncover insights about:

- AI adoption across industries and company sizes  
- Salary trends in relation to AI adoption  
- Remote work patterns in AI-influenced roles  
- Automation risk and future job growth  
- Skills demand and compensation differences  
- Identification of **future-proof roles** in an AI-driven economy  

The analysis is **descriptive and exploratory**, focused on understanding patterns in the data rather than building predictive models.

All analysis was performed using **Microsoft Excel** with the help of **pivot tables, charts, slicers, and feature engineering**.

## Key Findings

### AI Adoption Landscape

**1. AI Adoption Across Industries**
- Manufacturing shows the **highest AI adoption**, followed by **Education** and **Technology**  
- Transportation has the **lowest AI adoption**

**2. AI Adoption by Company Size & Industry**
- **High AI Adoption:**  
  - Healthcare (Large)  
  - Finance (Medium)  
  - Manufacturing (Small)  

- **Medium AI Adoption:**  
  - Technology (Large)  
  - Retail (Medium)  
  - Technology (Small)  

- **Low AI Adoption:**  
  - Manufacturing (Large)  
  - Education (Medium)  
  - Energy & Telecommunications (Small)

**3. Salary Trends by AI Adoption Level**
- Low AI adoption roles account for **~34%** of salary share  
- Medium and High AI adoption roles each account for **~32%**  
- **Lower AI adoption roles show ~2% higher salary share** than medium and high adoption roles  

---

### Workforce & Remote Trends

**4. Remote Friendliness of AI-Adopting Roles**
- **San Francisco** has the most AI-friendly remote jobs  
- Followed by **Singapore** and **Sydney**

**5. Job Roles Most Influenced by AI**
- **Highly influenced:** Data Scientist  
- **Moderately influenced:** HR Manager  
- **Low influence:** UX/UI Designer  

---

### Risk & Future Outlook

**6. Automation Risk vs AI Adoption**
- **High AI adoption** ‚Üí Medium automation risk  
- **Medium AI adoption** ‚Üí High automation risk  
- **Low AI adoption** ‚Üí Low automation risk  

**7. Job Growth Projections vs AI Adoption**
- **High AI adoption** ‚Üí Tends toward job growth decline  
- **Medium AI adoption** ‚Üí Mixed outcomes (decline or growth)  
- **Low AI adoption** ‚Üí More likely to be stable or growing  

---

### Skills & Compensation

**8. Skills Driving AI Adoption**
- **High AI adoption:** Marketing, Python, Cybersecurity  
- **Medium AI adoption:** Sales, Data Analysis, Machine Learning  
- **Low AI adoption:** Project Management, Python, Sales  

**9. Skill-Based Salary Differences**
- Higher-paying skills include:  
  **JavaScript, Python, Sales, Machine Learning**

## Key Takeaway

AI adoption is reshaping the job market in complex ways. **Higher AI usage does not always guarantee higher salaries or better job security**. Automation risk, skill demand, company size, and industry context all matter. A **multi-factor analysis** is essential to identify truly future-proof roles in an AI-driven economy.
